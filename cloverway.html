02 · The Cloverway Way

The operating principles
we still run on.

For sixteen years, one Long Beach office was the reason Japan's most iconic animation reached the Americas. That office — and its operating principles — is the foundation IP Bay is built on.

Toei Animation · Americas

Cloverway served as the official representative office of Toei Animation for the entire Americas — the licensed gateway for Toei's catalog into the United States, Latin America, and Brazil from 1991 until Toei opened its own U.S. subsidiary in 2004.

02
Shueisha & Viz · Intermediary

Viz Communications manga volumes of the era carried the explicit credit line: "English translation rights in the United States of America and Canada arranged by Shueisha, Inc. through Cloverway Inc." — a documented record of institutional trust with Japan's largest publisher.

03
Author-First · Uncut

Where most Western distributors of the era cut, recut, and rewrote Japanese animation to fit domestic formats, Cloverway refused. The Spanish-language Dragon Ball Z dub produced through Intertrack became regarded as among the most faithful anime dubs ever made for any Western market.

04
Two Continents · One Operator

Cloverway placed properties with Viz, Tokyopop, Pioneer, Bandai, ADV in the U.S.; and Televisa, TV Azteca, Turner, Cartoon Network, Telemundo, and Univision across Latin America and the U.S. Hispanic market. One operator, two continents, sixteen years.

Dragon Ball Z · Cloverway's Role

The definitive Latin American dub — and the Shueisha arrangement behind it.

Dragon Ball Z is among the most commercially significant anime series in history — produced by Toei Animation, based on Akira Toriyama's manga published by Shueisha. Cloverway held the Latin American distribution and production rights and commissioned the Spanish-language dub through Intertrack in Mexico City.

The Intertrack cast became legends in Latin American voice acting. The dub aired on Cartoon Network Latin America and across dozens of national broadcasters. Cloverway also syndicated the Spanish-language DBZ to Telemundo in the U.S., placing the series in front of American Hispanic households throughout the late '90s and early 2000s.

Cloverway's role as the formal intermediary between Shueisha and Viz for U.S. publishing licensing is preserved on the copyright pages of an entire generation of manga volumes.

Sailor Moon · Cloverway's Role

Two English seasons, one complete Latin American franchise.

Cloverway produced the English-language dubs of Sailor Moon S (Season 3) and Sailor Moon SuperS (Season 4) in association with Optimum Productions in Canada — the only two Sailor Moon seasons whose English versions were made directly by Cloverway. The credit appears onscreen at the end of each episode.

Across Latin America, Cloverway distributed and co-produced the complete Sailor Moon franchise — Seasons 1 through Sailor Stars, including the theatrical films — in both Spanish and Brazilian Portuguese.

Anime News Network's encyclopedia records Cloverway with formal Adaptation (1996, 1997, 1998), Dubbing, Production, and Special Thanks credits across the full Sailor Moon franchise.
