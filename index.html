Our Foundation

IP Bay is not a new
entrant to this business.
It built it.

In 1991, Yasuo Matsuo founded Cloverway Inc. in Long Beach, California — becoming one of the first people in the world to operate as an independent intermediary between Japanese animation studios and Western audiences.

Sailor Moon, Dragon Ball Z, Saint Seiya, Kinnikuman. These were not collectibles. These were the active inventory of a company placing Japan's most beloved properties on television screens across two continents.

Before there was a market for anime in the Americas, Yasuo was building one.

Yasuo Matsuo — Chairman

The trusted intermediary
for Japan's most important
publishers.

Through Cloverway, Yasuo served as the official representative of Toei Animation for the entire Americas — the sole gateway through which some of the most important Japanese IP of the 20th century reached Western screens.

His company acted as trusted partner for Shueisha, Shogakukan, Nippon Animation, and Tezuka Productions, placing properties with Viz Communications, Tokyopop, Pioneer Entertainment, and Bandai in the U.S. — and with Televisa, TV Azteca, Cartoon Network, and Telemundo across Latin America.

"For major Japanese producers including Shueisha and Nippon Animation, Cloverway was the company they trusted to guide them through an impenetrable market they didn't know anything about."

His approach never wavered: preserve the creative integrity of the original work. Cloverway refused to censor the properties it distributed when most Western distributors cut and rewrote content entirely.

It Runs in the Family

Jun Matsuo grew up
in this world.
He was born into it.

That Dragon Ball Z poster on the wall wasn't decoration. It was the inventory. Jun Matsuo grew up during the Cloverway years — surrounded by the properties his father was actively placing on television networks across two continents.

Today, as CEO of IP Bay, Jun leads day-to-day publisher engagement in Tokyo. He manages the Kadokawa relationship, leads presentations to rights divisions, and is the primary interface between IP Bay and Japan's major publishers.

What his father built through Cloverway, Jun carries forward — with the fluency of someone who never had to learn this world from the outside.

A Legacy of Respect

Kinnikuman was part of
Cloverway's legacy.
And always will be.

This is Jun Matsuo, Yasuo Matsuo, and Shimada-sensei — one of the creators of Kinnikuman, the manga with over 75 million copies in print.

At IP Bay, the author's vision comes first — always. We work closely with Japanese creators, week in and week out in Tokyo, because we understand that these stories belong to the people who made them. Respect for the author is not a courtesy. It is the foundation.

Yasuo built his entire career on one principle: the creative integrity of the original work is non-negotiable. That principle is the heart of everything IP Bay does.

Japan Access

Institutional access to
Japan's creative economy.

IP Bay's Japanese corporate entity and government-connected partner network provide direct access to Japan's most significant production incentive programs — and to the institutional relationships that make co-production possible.

Through a partner who holds a seat within the Cool Japan Initiative — Japan's national strategy for promoting its cultural content globally — IP Bay operates with government-level access that most Western companies cannot reach independently.

Cool Japan · METI · JETRO · Tokyo
Production Incentive
50% Cash Rebate
Japan's Content Incentive Program — launched by METI in 2023 and extended through 2026 — offers up to 50% cash back on qualifying production expenditures made in Japan. IP Bay's Japanese corporate entity provides direct gateway access to this rebate.
National Initiative
Cool Japan
Led by Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI), the Cool Japan Initiative promotes Japanese cultural IP — anime, manga, film, and games — on a global stage. IP Bay's partner holds a formal seat within this initiative, aligning IP Bay with Japan's official content export strategy.
Trade Organization
JETRO
Through the Japan External Trade Organization's Tokyo office, IP Bay accesses co-production resources, market intelligence, and official support channels for inbound and outbound content projects between Japan and the United States. JETRO operates 76 offices across 50 countries.
Tax Incentives
Prefectural Support
Japan's prefectural governments and the Visual Industry Promotion Organization (VIPO) administer supplemental location and production incentives for qualifying shoots across Japan's 6,800+ islands. IP Bay's in-country relationships provide access to regional programs that compound with national incentives.
