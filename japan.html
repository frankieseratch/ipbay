Production Incentive
50% Cash Rebate
Japan's Content Incentive Program — launched by METI in 2023 and extended through 2026 — offers up to 50% cash back on qualifying production expenditures made in Japan. IP Bay's Japanese corporate entity provides direct gateway access to this rebate.
National Initiative
Cool Japan · ¥20T by 2033
Overhauled in 2024 and led by Cabinet Minister Kimi Onoda under the Cabinet Office's IP Strategy Headquarters, Cool Japan targets ¥20 trillion (~$130B+) in overseas content revenue by 2033 — with the U.S. as primary market. Sakamoto sits inside the platform alongside Kodansha, Kadokawa, Toei, Shochiku, Toho, and Bandai Namco affiliates.
Trade Organization
JETRO
Through the Japan External Trade Organization's Tokyo office, IP Bay accesses co-production resources, market intelligence, and official support channels for inbound and outbound content projects between Japan and the United States. JETRO operates 76 offices across 50 countries.
Tax Incentives
Prefectural Support
Japan's prefectural governments and the Visual Industry Promotion Organization (VIPO) administer supplemental location and production incentives for qualifying shoots across Japan's 6,800+ islands. IP Bay's in-country relationships provide access to regional programs that compound with national incentives.
The Publisher Effect

Why This Matters

Government endorsement changes what Japan's publishers are willing to do.

In Japanese business culture, government endorsement carries institutional weight that no amount of Hollywood credits can replicate. A government-linked introduction signals that working with IP Bay is aligned with Japan's national interest — not just a commercial proposition.

Publishers are far more likely to engage seriously on multi-property deals when a government relationship is in place. This is the difference between a publisher saying yes to one property versus opening a multi-property conversation.

What the Government Is Looking For

A Hollywood voice inside Japan's ¥20T content export strategy.

Japan's core challenge in executing Cool Japan is the U.S. market. They need substantive Hollywood expertise — someone who can look at specific Japanese IP and tell them precisely what will work, what needs to change, and how to build a franchise from it.

IP Bay delivers that voice — with the publisher trust Yasuo built, the on-the-ground Tokyo operation Jun runs, and the government-registered channel Sakamoto owns.
